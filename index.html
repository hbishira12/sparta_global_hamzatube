Lion and cubs

Just like human children, life for a lion cub is all about play. and above shows the male lion whilst the cubs play. So much more than just a fun way to pass the time, play also helps little lions develop and grow. By running, climbing and wrestling, they practice their gross motor skills and develop physical strength and coordination.

Tiger cubs amongst one another

The cubs showing there bond with one another, and are showing their personalities, which is inspiring to watch. looking very laid back, just like his father Malosi. You might notice that one pf the tigers has two parallel lines inside a stripe on his head that resemble a pause button.

Lions lying and sitting

The endangered Sumatran lion cubs lying and sitting. Male lions spend 18 to 20 hours a day snoozing, while females get 15 to 18 hours of shuteye. The lionesses spend more time hunting and taking care of cubs, which is why they get slightly less sleep.
